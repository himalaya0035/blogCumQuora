Amy Barker

Lives in Kentucky

What surprised you today?

I recently started working night shift in a factory. Before I started, I was told by practically everyone that I asked, “You will be working with gruff men, men’s men. Many have worked there for decades. You’ll be hazed by those men with seniority. Just take it in stride, keep your head down, and learn. You’ll be accepted eventually.” Believe it or not, that was said almost verbatim in the phone interview and also implied in orientation. Needless to say, I was more than a little nervous when I first got on the humongous floor and saw only one other woman already working if I craned my neck. By my first night, I had found that this talk of hazing couldn’t be further from the truth.
